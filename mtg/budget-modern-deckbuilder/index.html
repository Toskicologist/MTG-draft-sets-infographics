Where do the prices come from? (TCG Mid & TCGcsv)

TCG Mid is TCGplayer's Mid price — the median of the current Near Mint / Lightly Played listings for a card. It's the "typical asking price" figure (distinct from TCGplayer's Market price, which is an average of recent completed sales).

TCGplayer's own pricing API is closed to new apps, so the Mid figure here comes from TCGcsv — a free, community-run mirror of TCGplayer's public pricing — refreshed once a day (~20:00 UTC). A scheduled job rebuilds the price table and a slim card index (printings, legalities, image) from Scryfall's bulk data, and publishes both to a CDN. The page loads those two files once, so Mid mode does zero per-card API calls and is near-instant. The banner above shows exactly when the data was built; every export carries that same timestamp.

Each card is matched to its TCGplayer product by Scryfall's tcgplayer_id (an exact ID match, not a name guess); the cheapest printing's Mid is used. Cards with no Mid — or not legal in the selected Format — are flagged, never estimated. Totals recompute automatically as you edit (no button). Anything missing from the daily index is flagged and can be resolved on demand with a one-click, throttled Scryfall lookup.
